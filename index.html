About

I am a student living in Cape Town, South Africa, currently working towards my Master's degree in Computer Science from UCT. I am passionate about artificial intelligence and computer games design, and fields that exist at the intersection of technology and psychology, such as gamification and Virtual Reality. Photography, philosophy, mastering the art of the yoyo, and playing computer games are some of my hobbies.

I have a BSocSci (Philosophy and Psychology), a BSc (Computer Science and Computer Games Design), and an Honours in Computer Science from UCT. I have worked as an IT developer intern at Zando, a South African online fashion store. I have also been working as a computer science tutor at UCT since 2014. Master's Research - User Experience in Virtual Reality

The visual cut-off created by Head Mounted Displays (HMDs), while affording a heightened sense of immersion, creates usability issues for other peripherals and objects.

This research aims to explore a way to alleviate these issues - Augmented Virtuality. By attaching a webcam (or similar device) to the front of the HMD, the user is able to "see through" the device when necessary, facilitating interaction with peripheral devices and other real-world objects.

  • Website: JHC Master's Research, Research Group
  • Date: January 2016 - Present
